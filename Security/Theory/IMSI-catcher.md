An **IMSI catcher** is a surveillance device used to intercept mobile phone signals and identify nearby devices.

### What “IMSI” means

- **IMSI** stands for _International Mobile Subscriber Identity_
- It’s a unique number stored on your SIM card that identifies you on a cellular network

### How an IMSI catcher works

An IMSI catcher (often called a **“Stingray”**, after a brand name) pretends to be a legitimate cell tower. Your phone automatically connects to it because it appears to offer a strong signal. Once connected, the device can:

- Collect IMSI numbers from phones nearby
- Track the location of those phones
- In some cases, intercept calls, texts, or data (depending on the device and network security)

### Who uses them

- Law enforcement and intelligence agencies
- Military organizations
- Sometimes used in investigations or surveillance operations

### Why they’re controversial

- They can capture data from **all phones in an area**, not just a specific target
- This raises concerns about privacy and legality in many countries

### Are you at risk?

For most people, encountering one is rare, but they’re sometimes used at large events, protests, or in targeted investigations.
